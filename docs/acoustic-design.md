# Acoustic and analog design — updated 2026-07-04

Operating frequency: **40 kHz** (ultrasonic pings). 
Sensor physical geometry: Down-facing bounce path (reflective plate layout, $200\text{ mm}$ physical path length).

---

## Link budget analysis

### TX Signal Level
*   **Acoustic source level:** Multicomp MCUSD16A40S12RO primary (or user waterproof spares). Typical transmit sensitivity: **$115\text{ dB}$ SPL** ref $20\text{ }\mu\text{Pa}$ at $1\text{ m}$ for $10\text{ Vrms}$ drive.
*   **Driver:** Direct $12\text{ Vpp}$ square wave ($4.24\text{ Vrms}$ fundamental). This drops SPL by only **$7.4\text{ dB}$** compared to a $30\text{ Vpp}$ drive.
*   **Source Level ($SL_{1m}$):** $115\text{ dB} - 7.4\text{ dB} \approx \mathbf{107.6\text{ dB}}$ SPL at $1\text{ m}$.

### Losses (200 mm Path Length)
*   **Geometric spreading:** $20 \log_{10}(0.2\text{ m}) = -\mathbf{14.0\text{ dB}}$.
*   **Atmospheric absorption:** $0.05\text{ dB}$ at $40\text{ kHz}$ over $200\text{ mm}$ (negligible).
*   **Reflection loss (Al reflector disc):** $-\mathbf{1.0\text{ dB}}$.
*   **Received sound pressure level ($SPL_{rx}$):** $107.6\text{ dB} - 14.0\text{ dB} - 1.0\text{ dB} \approx \mathbf{92.6\text{ dB}}$ SPL at the receiver transducer.

### RX Signal Level
*   **Transducer receive sensitivity:** $-65\text{ dB}$ ref $1\text{ V}/\mu\text{bar}$ (equivalent to $-105\text{ dB}$ ref $1\text{ V}/\text{Pa}$).
*   **Sound pressure in Pascals:** $92.6\text{ dB}$ SPL $\approx \mathbf{0.85\text{ Pa}}$ RMS.
*   **Open-circuit voltage ($V_{oc}$):**
    $$V_{oc} = 0.85\text{ Pa} \times 10^{-105/20}\text{ V/Pa} \approx 4.8\text{ mV RMS} \approx \mathbf{13.5\text{ mVpp}}$$
*   **Minimum signal (under wind attenuation / spray):** Assume a worst-case attenuation of $-20\text{ dB}$ (due to beam deflection or spray on reflector). $V_{oc,min} \approx \mathbf{1.35\text{ mVpp}}$.

## Front end

### TX
*   Driven at **$12\text{ Vpp}$** directly from the $12\text{ V}$ protected input.
*   **IXDF602SI** dual gate driver buffers the 40 kHz PWM from the ESP32-S3.
*   **DG412DY** quad SPST switch selects the active transducer. Inactive channels are isolated by opening the switch ($C_{off} \approx 5\text{ pF}$, $R_{off} \approx \text{Gigaohms}$), allowing the transducer to float freely as a receiver.
*   **TX marker:** Injected from the logic-side `PWM_A` signal via a $3.3\text{ M}\Omega$ resistor into the post-mux summing node, placing the $t_0$ reference directly in the capture buffer.

### RX
*   Signal path: transducer → $10\text{ k}\Omega$ series + BAV99 clamp → **74HC4051** 8:1 multiplexer → AC couple (1 nF) → fixed-gain stage A1 ($+40\text{ dB}$, G=101) with bandpass filtering → Sallen-Key $48\text{ kHz}$ low-pass filter (A2) → $1\text{ }\mu\text{F}$ AC-coupling capacitor → PCM1808 ADC input VINL.
*   **Fixed Gain:** A single $+40\text{ dB}$ fixed gain stage is used. The 24-bit delta-sigma ADC has a dynamic range of $>98\text{ dB}$ SNR (noise floor $\approx 5\text{ }\mu\text{V}$), which easily resolves signals from $1\text{ mVpp}$ up to $3\text{ Vpp}$ without needing programmable gain amplifiers or switches.
*   **Op-Amp:** A single **TLV9062** dual 10 MHz rail-to-rail op-amp chip (SOIC-8) is used for the entire analog chain (A1 + A2).

### ADC + capture
*   **I2S Stereo ADC (PCM1808, TSSOP-14):** Natively supported by the ESP32-S3's I2S peripheral using standard DMA.
*   **Sample Rate:** Run at **$96\text{ kSPS}$** — clock generated by the ESP32-S3's internal PLL. MCLK ($256 \times F_s = 24.576\text{ MHz}$), BCK, and LRCK are output from the ESP32-S3, keeping the ADC synchronized.
*   **Capture Window:** 1.2 ms = 115 samples (approx 460 bytes at 32-bit slot width). 
*   **Pin Cost:** Only 4 pins (MCLK, BCLK, LRCK, SDOUT). This frees up 11 GPIO pins compared to the parallel ADC, allowing the control shift registers to be completely eliminated.
*   **Group Delay:** The PCM1808 delta-sigma modulator group delay is symmetric and cancels out perfectly in the reciprocal time-of-flight difference $\Delta t$.

## Measurement cycle

*   Slot 1.5 ms per direction (373 µs flight + ring-down margin; sealed transducers are high-Q — the just-fired element needs settling time before its own RX slot).
*   Sequence N→S, S→N, E→W, W→E; 32 pings per direction averaged → **5 Hz output**, per-sample resolution well under 0.05 m/s.

---

## Correlation algorithm (firmware)

1. Find TX marker → t0 on the capture's own clock.
2. Mix to baseband (40 kHz I/Q complex envelope). No decimation needed due to the low 96 kSPS sampling rate.
3. Coarse ToF: envelope correlation against a template (exponentially averaged real echoes, self-calibrating).
4. Fine ToF: carrier phase of the complex correlation at the coarse peak. Cycle-slip guard: envelope timing must be good to ±12.5 µs (half cycle) — holds above ~20 dB SNR.
5. Reciprocal formulas → v_axis, c. Two axes → speed + direction.
6. Apply IMU motion compensation and Magnetometer heading reference.
7. Direction calibration LUT for wake/geometry effects applied in firmware.

Compute: ~115-sample complex correlation over ±12 lags ≈ negligible CPU usage on the ESP32-S3, easily fitting within the slot budget.
